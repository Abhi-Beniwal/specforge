GDPR - General Data Protection Regulation Summary

GDPR applies to any product that collects or processes personal data of EU residents.

Key principles:
- Lawful basis: You must have a legal reason to collect data. Common bases are user consent, contract fulfillment, or legitimate interest.
- Data minimisation: Only collect data you actually need. Do not collect extra fields just in case.
- Purpose limitation: Data collected for one purpose cannot be used for another without fresh consent.
- Storage limitation: Do not store personal data longer than necessary. Define retention periods.
- Right to erasure: Users can request their data be deleted. Your system must support this.
- Right to access: Users can request a copy of all data you hold on them.
- Data breach notification: If a breach occurs, you must notify the relevant authority within 72 hours.
- Data Protection Officer: Required if you process sensitive data at large scale.
- Privacy by design: Security and privacy must be built into the system from the start, not added later.

Sensitive data categories requiring extra protection:
- Health and medical data
- Biometric data
- Financial data
- Children's data (under 13 in most jurisdictions)

Fines for non-compliance: Up to 4% of global annual revenue or 20 million euros, whichever is higher.

For AI products specifically:
- Automated decision-making that significantly affects users requires explicit consent.
- Users must be informed when they are interacting with an AI system.
- Profiling users requires a lawful basis and must be disclosed in privacy policy.
