DPDP - Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (India)

India's DPDP Act came into effect in 2023 and applies to all products processing digital personal data of Indian residents.

Key obligations for businesses (called Data Fiduciaries):
- Consent: Must obtain free, informed, specific, and unambiguous consent before processing personal data.
- Purpose limitation: Data can only be used for the purpose for which consent was given.
- Data minimisation: Collect only what is necessary for the stated purpose.
- Accuracy: Take reasonable steps to ensure data is accurate and up to date.
- Storage limitation: Delete personal data once the purpose is fulfilled.
- Security: Implement reasonable security safeguards to prevent data breaches.

Children's data (under 18 in India):
- Parental or guardian consent is mandatory before processing data of anyone under 18.
- Behavioural monitoring and targeted advertising to children is prohibited.
- Age verification mechanisms are required.

User rights:
- Right to access: Users can ask what data is held about them.
- Right to correction: Users can request correction of inaccurate data.
- Right to erasure: Users can request deletion of their data.
- Right to grievance redressal: Every platform must have a grievance officer.

Penalties:
- Up to 250 crore rupees for failure to implement security safeguards.
- Up to 200 crore rupees for failure to notify data breaches.
- Up to 50 crore rupees for violating children's data provisions.

For AI and SaaS products targeting Indian users:
- A clear privacy policy in simple language is mandatory.
- Consent must be obtained before onboarding, not buried in terms and conditions.
- Data localisation rules are still being finalised but sensitive data may need to stay in India.
